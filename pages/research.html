Research 1 : Navigation Bar
Navigation bar is one of the most important element while designing a website because it allows users to quickly visit any section within the site. A website without a navigation bar becomes very hard to keep track of. The most common used type of navigation bar is fixed or navigation bar. It is basically a bar stapled to the top of the screen. So, the users will never have to scroll to the top just to navigate to other pages. To make the users stick to a page, they must find every aspect of the webpage intersting and the concept of fixed navbar enhances such experience by saving time and giving user control.

Both of the websites referenced by the images has used fixed navigation bar to uplift user experience. Although their designs are different, they have the same task i.e to stick to the top and make navigating to other pages easier
Research 2 : Footer
Footers are as important as the navigation bar in a website. They are not simply a place to put your copyright notice and certainly not the end of a website. They can to used to display important features such as brief information of the creator. They can be also used to enable linking to social apps such as linkedin, instagram and others to increase networking. So rather than making a small footer, it would be smart to add contents to the footer to make the website more navigatable.

Footer referenced by both these images are similar to a great extent. The footer in both websites have been divided into two section where the first section contains columns relating to contact details and useful links while the second contains copyright details.
Research 3 : Dynamic Div
Proper use of JavaScript and css styles can lead to an appealing and clean changing of content in a webpage. It takes less time to load than creating a whole page and changing the contents of a particular part. Use of display properties is changed when a button is pressed and changed the property changes from block to none or vice versa.

Design of both the webpages are completely different.The right website uses the concept to change some content while in the right major part of the page is changed.So, both of them have the same motive: to change the content dynamically. It has been achieved using JavaScript. Rather than redirecting to a different page to view differnt contents it uses Javascript to change the style of the html div.
Research 4 : Contact Page
Contact page of a website holds same significance as other pages.The first function of a contact page is to grab attention of viewers. The concept is basically to let the users share their ideas regarding the contents of the webpage. In majority of the time, it is visited after all other contents are viewed. But a poorly managed contact page will surely bore the viewer despite the brilliance of other pages. Since user feedback is a important aspect to develop the pages, its important to keep the contact page simple, straightforward and as clean as possible.

The contact page of both the images are almost identical to one other. The major similarities are that both of them have a image and a form next to each other. Altough thier position are alternate. Both of them have a plain background and a submit button along with input fields
Research 5: Landing Page
The first thing one notices when visiting a website is the landing page. Landing page is basically the first web page that appears when a user clicks on a search engine result link. Landing page is the selling point of a website because it determines whether the user will stick to the page or move on to other interesting pages. A viewer may most certainly prefer a simple, clean and attractive landing page to one filled with lots of contents.

Landing pages of both the images are identical to some extent. Both of them are simple and clean with a navbar with navigation options aligned to left. The website represented by left image however lacks logo. The section below the navbar is same with some texts, images and buttons. However the position of the image is alternate in the pages
